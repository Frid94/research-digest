## Burial Archaeology & Mortuary Practice

**The Herlaugshaugen ship burial: closing the gap between the East Anglian and Scandinavian ship burial traditions**
*Geir Grønnesby, Hanne Bryn, Lars Forseth (2026) — Antiquity*
New analysis of the Norwegian Herlaugshaugen ship burial examining connections between Scandinavian and East Anglian burial traditions.
Link: https://doi.org/10.15184/aqy.2026.10330

**The Late Iron Age Mound Raknehaugen in Norway: A Ritual Response to the Sixth-Century Crisis**
*Lars Gustavsen (2026) — European Journal of Archaeology*
Interprets the monumental Raknehaugen mound as a ritual response to the sixth-century climatic and social crisis — directly relevant to Late Iron Age mortuary monumentalism.
Link: https://doi.org/10.1017/eaa.2025.10026

**Flat white or with a round character? New light on stone funerary slabs and orbs from south Sweden**
*Ing-Marie Back Danielsson (2026) — World Archaeology*
New interpretation of funerary stone objects from south Sweden, engaging with materiality and mortuary practice in the Late Iron Age.
Link: https://doi.org/10.1080/00438243.2026.2650141

**The Rosette Fibulas and the Social Strategies of the Late Roman Iron Age**
*Torben Trier Christiansen (2026) — AmS-Skrifter*
Analysis of rosette fibulae as markers of social strategy — part of a new 2026 AmS-Skrifter volume (see Norse Religion section).
Link: https://doi.org/10.31265/ecfh7q94

**Gripping Boundaries**
*Mats Skare (2025) — Danish Journal of Archaeology*
Link: https://doi.org/10.7146/dja.v14i1.156972

## Norse Religion & Ritual

> **Note:** A new 2026 volume of *AmS-Skrifter* (Archaeological Museum Stavanger) contains multiple articles on Late Iron Age iconography and ritual. Worth tracking down the full volume.

**Crampons – Late Iron-age everyday items and/or artefacts with sacred meaning?**
*Anne-Sofie Gräslund (2026) — AmS-Skrifter*
Gräslund examines iron crampons as potentially ritual objects — characteristic of her work on the boundary between everyday life and sacred meaning in the Late Iron Age.
Link: https://doi.org/10.31265/y4cp9j58

**Convergence of knowledge**
*Unn Pedersen, Elna Siv Kristoffersen, Nathalie Hanna (2026) — AmS-Skrifter*
Link: https://doi.org/10.31265/mxaz8m12

**On the iconography of the gold foil figures from Hauge**
*Sigmund Oehrl (2026) — AmS-Skrifter*
Iconographic study of gold foil figures (guldgubber) — significant for understanding Late Iron Age ritual imagery and cult sites.
Link: https://doi.org/10.31265/ndsdkk69

## Multispecies Archaeology & Birds

**The Raven's Eye. Stylistic references to sight and vision as symbols of knowledge and power**
*Anne Nørgård Jørgensen, Sofie Laurine Albris (2026) — AmS-Skrifter*
Examines raven iconography in Late Iron Age art — directly relevant to avian symbolism and human-bird relations in the period.
Link: https://doi.org/10.31265/cnpbxx13

**Waterbirds, mustelids and bast fibres – evidence of soft organic materials in the Late Mesolithic Skateholm I and II cemeteries, Sweden**
*Tuija Kirkinen, Lars Larsson, Kristiina Mannermaa (2026) — Archaeological and Anthropological Sciences*
Identifies waterbird remains (feathers/skins) as burial goods in Swedish Mesolithic cemeteries. Earlier period but directly relevant to the long tradition of birds in Scandinavian burial contexts. Mannermaa is a key scholar on this topic.
Link: https://doi.org/10.1007/s12520-026-02415-7

**Seal Hunters of the Archipelago**
*Teemu Väisänen, Janne Soisalo (2026) — Muinaistutkija*
Finnish-language publication on seal hunting in the archipelago — potentially relevant for Ålandic/Baltic maritime subsistence contexts.
Link: https://doi.org/10.61258/mt.161393

## Zooarchaeology

**The Introduction of Chickens Into Norway and Their Early Use: New Evidence From the Borgund Kaupang**
*Samuel J. Walker, Hanneke J. M. Meijer, Julia Best (2025) — International Journal of Osteoarchaeology*
New zooarchaeological evidence for chicken introduction and use in medieval Norway. Highly relevant given the role of domestic chicken in mainland Late Iron Age burial assemblages.
Link: https://doi.org/10.1002/oa.70018
